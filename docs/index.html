Dynamic Emotion Wheel

The Dynamic Emotion Wheel is an emotion self-report and awareness tool primarily thought for research in the fields of Computer-Supported Collabrative Learning and Affective Sciences.

About

The Dynamic Emotion Wheel (DEW) is a web application allowing users to report and share their emotions during a computer-mediated task.

  • Inspired by the Geneva Emotion Wheel, the tool combines the two most widely adopted approaches to emotion self-report: the dimensional and discrete emotions approaches.
  • Rooted in the Component Process Model theoretical framework, the tool adopts appraisal criteria and subjective feeling both for self-assessment and data visualization.
  • Appraisal and subjective feelings are directly linked: the tool computes a subset of "most likely" discrete emotions according to a quantitative evaluation on two-dimensional criteria (e.g. Valence and Power).
  • Participants to a collaborative task can follow both their own and their partner's evolution of emotional episodes over time (awareness).
  • The tool is meant to be flexible and >extensible to different experimental contexts and applications.

A prototype of the tool has been developed as part of the Emotion Awareness Tools for Mediated Interaction (EATMINT) project, whose aims included the development of emotion awareness tools (EAT) to improve the collaborative processes and outcomes of people working together through computers.

Research Toolbox

The Research Toolbox, currently in beta testing, is a user-friendly environment where researchers can customize and set-up a version of the DEW according to their needs and interests.

Ad admin panel allows to organize the various aspects of the toolbox.

It is possible to create different studies and export the data for analysis.

Researchers can choose or create their own underlying affective spaces.
